Our Mission To Clean and Restore Judson Lake

Judson Lake is an important ecosystem, strategically located along the Pacific Flyway, playing host to more than sixty bird species including the Peregrine Falcon, Bald Eagle and several hawk and waterfowl species. Sadly, this wetland is in a severely degraded state as progressively receding water levels have facilitated the decrease of once abundant fish populations, the spread of non-native noxious weeds (Purple Loosestrife) and the suffering lead poisoning deaths of far too many majestic Trumpeter Swans.

However, the dry lake floor during the summer months provides us the perfect opportunity to remove the toxic lead shot that has accumulated over decades.

How We'll Do It

On behalf of Willock Farms, Walter Willms wants to conduct a project to dig Judson Lake deeper. This would provide many benefits for the wildlife residing in Judson Lake. For one, the shores and deepened lake grounds will be replaced with fresh ground. Because of this, the trumpeter swans will be once again able to live healthy lives and swallow grit/rocks in the land without risk of death from the lead bullets which have been banned since 1990.

Not only would this project save the swans, it would also save the wildlife living within the body of water, thereby saving the hawks and eagles which eat the different lake species. Judson Lake as of now is approximately seven feet deep. If Walter's plan succeeds, the lake will end up being approximately 40-50 feet deep. Once this is done, it would be nearly impossible for the lake to dry up, and the lilypads and excessive weeds will not reach the surface of the lake any longer. In result, the fish will not suffer a residence that is too small for them to live in.
